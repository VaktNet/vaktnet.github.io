Guy Teets

Cyber-Physical Systems & Infrastructure Security Leader

VaktNet began as a mission-driven company—DataVakt LLC—a veteran-led effort launched in the aftermath of everything that was 2020, a year that felt like a long decade. It was born to bring enterprise-grade security and resilient networks to small and mid-sized businesses, nonprofits, and veteran service organizations during a time of rising threats and unstable markets. Though the company dissolved during my tenure at Disney due to global supply constraints, the ethos behind it lives on: infrastructure worth trusting, systems designed with vigilance, and leadership grounded in integrity.

I’ve led OT/ICS cybersecurity initiatives across world-class themed entertainment destinations, built secure infrastructure at Department of Defense scale, and delivered high-availability systems across mission-critical environments. This site reflects that journey—what I’ve built, what I’ve learned, and how I lead.

Featured Projects

Epic Universe Parkwide Wireless Deployment
Directed the design, installation, and activation of Wi-Fi 6, DAS, two-way radio, and emergency responder systems across a 750+ acre theme park resort. Led RF spectrum coordination and wireless system compliance from construction through test & adjust.

Disney OT Cybersecurity – Rise of the Resistance & Millennium Falcon
Led vulnerability discovery and mitigation efforts for complex OT/ICS ride systems. Served as field SME for the Fluidmesh (now Cisco CURWB) wireless mesh network during system test & adjust at Walt Disney World and Disneyland.

Disney OT Security Architecture – Guardians of the Galaxy & Tiana’s Bayou Adventure
Designed OT security architecture including Layer 1–3 segmentation, firewall rulesets, and log aggregation integration. Co-authored the AOT Security Policy Standard (AOTSPS), now adopted across future Walt Disney Destinations & Experiences projects—including new builds, refurbishments, and as a model for adjacent business units such as Disney Cruise Line (DCL), Walt Disney Studios, and ESPN.

DISA MPLS / JRSS Global Upgrade
Delivered TSIPs, BOMs, and readiness packages for 27 global DISA facilities. Created a time-zone coordination workflow that cut field delays by 50% and reduced deployment durations by up to 10 days across PACOM, EUCOM, and CENTCOM.
