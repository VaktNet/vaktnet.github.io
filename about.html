Origin Story of VaktNet

What even is a Vakt?

A Vakt is a Norwegian word defined as “a person employed to look after someone or something” by the Cambridge Dictionary. It’s more simply defined as a guard.

In 2020, a year that felt like a decade, I started DataVakt, a small business intended to help other small and medium sized businesses and non‑profit organizations improve their cybersecurity posture and upgrade their wired and wireless networks. By the middle of 2021 the global landscape had shifted greatly. Those organizations I intended to help no longer had the financial overhead to engage DataVakt’s services, and my own time was increasingly occupied by projects at Walt Disney World.

The DataVakt Shield

The shield embodies the idea that technology and culture must be protected. Inspired by Viking heritage and the trinity knot, it represents layers of defense—digital, operational, and human.

The company was shelved, but the idea and the spirit of DataVakt—a guardian of data and systems—live on. The vision has even expanded: making the scary, annoying, and often complex boogiemen of cybersecurity, IT, and mentorship understandable, manageable, and available to everyone.

VaktNet is my portfolio page, building on the name Vakt from that business. DataVakt itself will be the tower in which a podcast will live—with the hope of launching speaking engagements and perhaps even reviving the business to provide enterprise‑level NOC/SOC service to SMBs and non‑profits as a cooperative network and security solution.
